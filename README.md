# Wind and Solar Powered Lighting and Charging System for Roads and Highways

This project presents a hybrid renewable energy system designed to power street lighting and electric vehicle charging infrastructure on roads and highways using wind and solar energy. The system operates completely off-grid, making it ideal for remote and underdeveloped regions.

## Overview

This is a **multidisciplinary project** that brings together concepts from mechanical, electrical, civil, and environmental engineering. The design combines energy generation, storage, and intelligent control in a single, sustainable infrastructure unit.

## System Architecture

| Component           | Function                                                    |
|--------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Solar Panel        | Converts sunlight into electrical energy                    |
| Wind Turbine       | Harvests wind energy to generate electricity                |
| Charge Controller  | Regulates energy flow to prevent overcharging               |
| Battery Storage    | Stores excess energy for use at night or during outages     |
| LED Streetlights   | Provide low-power lighting along roadways                   |
| EV Charging Port   | Supplies energy for electric vehicle charging               |
| Sensors            | Automate lighting based on motion and ambient light         |

## Methodology

- Site Analysis
Evaluated sunlight exposure, wind direction, vehicle traffic patterns, and pole positioning to maximize energy capture.

- System Design
Combined wind and solar subsystems into a single infrastructure unit. Designed vertical-axis turbines and selected appropriate solar panels.

- Electrical Circuit Design
Designed the charge controller circuit and integrated protection mechanisms to prevent overcharging and short circuits.

- Model Testing & Prototyping
Created a scaled prototype using basic electronic components, tested voltage/current outputs, and verified charging and lighting functionality.
